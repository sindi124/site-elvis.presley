Early Life

Elvis Presley was born on January 8, 1935, in Tupelo, Mississippi. From humble beginnings, he was influenced by gospel music and began performing at a young age.

Rise to Fame

Elvis signed with RCA Records in 1955, and his first hit single "Heartbreak Hotel" in 1956 made him a national sensation. His combination of rock, country, and gospel music redefined American pop culture.

Later Years and Legacy

Elvis continued recording and performing throughout the 60s and 70s, becoming one of the best-selling solo artists of all time. He passed away in 1977 but remains a cultural icon.
